System Calibration

After installing your home theater system, the next important step is to calibrate it. Calibration involves using the built-in test tone generator in your surround processor to adjust each speaker's output, ensuring they are equally loud when heard from the viewing/listening position. You can estimate the levels by ear, which can yield satisfactory results, or you can use a sound pressure meter for more precise measurements. Follow the calibration instructions provided with your surround processor to guide you. The goal of calibration is to create a cohesive sound field where the front and surround speakers work together harmoniously. It's common for people to increase the surround gain until the surround speakers become a distinct source. However, this is generally not ideal as overly loud surrounds can distract from the screen and diminish the immersive experience. While adjusting the levels, it's important to avoid turning the surrounds up to match the loudness of the front speakers. Directors and sound engineers typically mix surround material at lower levels compared to the front channels intentionally. By calibrating your system properly and respecting the intended level balance, you can achieve an immersive and balanced sound experience.
